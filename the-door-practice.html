the door

A quiet practice for finding your next step.

1. Ground

Settle into presence. Take three slow breaths. Feel the weight of your body. Notice that you are here, in this moment, about to turn your attention toward something.

What is present for you right now — not what you think about your situation, but what you actually feel in this moment?

1 of 4

2. Name

Without judging it, without trying to fix it — what is the thing that is actually here? Not the story about it, not what you wish it were. Just the shape of it.

If you had to name what is alive for you right now in a single phrase, what would it be?

2 of 4

3. Direction

Not the whole path. Not the five-year plan. Just: what wants to happen? What is the quality of movement that feels true — toward something, away from something, a pause, a turn, a letting go?

What direction, however small, feels like it has life in it?

3 of 4

4. Step

The smallest possible next thing. Not the big action. Not the commitment. Just: what is one thing you could do, in the next hour or today, that moves in that direction?

It can be absurdly small. A single email. A five-minute walk. Opening a document. Saying one sentence. The size does not matter. The direction does.

4 of 4

What you found

These are not answers you produced. They are what was already here, given space to show itself.

Your next step

That step is enough. You do not need to know what comes after it. The door only opens one way — forward, into the next moment. You are already there.
