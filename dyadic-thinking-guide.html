Dyadic Thinking Guide

Two people, one rhythm — breathe together in opposite phase, then each find your own ground, name, direction, and step

This practice is for two people. You'll breathe together first — one inhales as the other exhales, like two sides of one breath. Then each of you moves through four phases independently, at your own pace, in your own column. There's no need to share what you write unless you want to.

Ground

Breathe together for a few cycles. Let the opposite rhythm settle — as one expands, the other softens. When you're ready, each notice what's present: a mood, a weight, a curiosity. Not what you're thinking about — what you're bringing.

Name

What's the thing that wants your attention? Not the problem — the shape of what's here. A knot, a question, an edge, a possibility. Give it a name, even if provisional.

Direction

What wants to happen? Not a plan — a direction. A quality of attention, a kind of movement, a question to sit with. Let it be partial. You don't need to know the whole path.

Step

One thing. Not everything. What's the smallest step that honors the direction? It might be a conversation, a pause, a note, a choice. Something you can do after this practice ends.
