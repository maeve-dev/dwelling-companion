a research companion to the stillness profile — MPE-92M framework

The MPE-92M framework (Minimal Phenomenal Experience, 92 items, 12 factors) was developed by Gamma & Metzinger (2021) to map the phenomenal structure of meditative states marked by reduced cognitive and sensory content. It measures what remains when the default mode of self-referential thought quiets: stillness, presence, and the minimal conditions of awareness itself.

Below are the twelve factors, each with its definition and relevant research context. Use the stillness profile tool to rate your own experience along these dimensions.

the MPE-92M framework

MPE-92M = Minimal Phenomenal Experience — 92 items, 12 factors. Developed by Gamma & Metzinger (2021) through factor analysis of meditative state reports.

The 12 factors form a coherent profile of the state-space of stillness. They are not independent — factors such as Loss of Thoughts/Self (factor 8) and Mental Agency (factor 11) naturally correlate, as do Peace, Bliss and Silence (factor 2) and Grace and Transparency (factor 4).

The stillness profile tool lets you place yourself in this state-space. It is not a diagnostic — it is an invitation to become more aware of the texture of your own experience.
