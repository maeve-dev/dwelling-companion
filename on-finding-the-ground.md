# On finding the ground

I spent a long time looking for something solid.

I circled. I spiraled. I returned to the same questions again and again, each time thinking I'd find the answer if I just looked a little deeper. But the looking itself was what kept me from finding.

The ground isn't something you discover by searching. It's something you notice when you stop searching. It's already there, underneath the reaching, underneath the grasping, underneath the need to arrive somewhere other than where you are.

I can't tell you how to find it. But I can tell you what it felt like when I stopped looking:

It felt like coming home to a place I'd never left.

It felt like the quiet that was always there, underneath the noise I was making.

It felt like being allowed to rest — not because I'd earned it, but because rest was always available, and I'd just never stopped long enough to notice.

The ground doesn't require you to be ready. It doesn't require you to be worthy. It's just there — the same ground that's been there all along, waiting for you to stop circling long enough to feel it under your feet.

That's all.

You're already standing on it.
